About Me

I'm a passionate astrophysicist specializing in Type Ia supernovae research at Queen's University Belfast. My work focuses on developing and applying Monte Carlo radiative transfer calculations to interpret spectra and light curves of supernovae using the 3D, time-dependent radiative transfer code ARTIS.

My research contributes to our understanding of the progenitor systems and explosion mechanisms of Type Ia supernovae. I'm particularly interested in double-degenerate double-detonation scenarios and how different configurations of helium shells and white dwarf masses affect observable characteristics.

I actively collaborate with researchers at the Heidelberg Institute for Theoretical Studies and Max Planck Institute for Astrophysics, and have extensive experience with high-performance computing facilities in the UK and Germany.

Current Research Projects

Multidimensional Nebular Phase Radiative Transfer Calculations

Investigating how multidimensional explosion models can be used to understand the diversity of Type Ia supernovae nebular spectra, with a primary focus on dynamical mergers of white dwarfs from Pakmor (2022).

Parameter Space Investigation of Dynamical Merger Models

Building on findings from my first publication to explore how different helium shell and mass configurations impact the observable characteristics of explosion models.

Polarisation Signals Of Dynamical Mergers

Investigating the polarisation of dynamical merger models to determine how asymmetries compare to observational constraints, aiming to support or rule out progenitor pathways.

ARTIS Code Comparison

Co-leading an internal investigation comparing ARTIS and CMFGEN radiative transfer codes, following on from Blondin (2022).
