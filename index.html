Hello there! I'm Josh Pollin

Graduate Student in Astrophysics
Queen's University Belfast

I'm a passionate astrophysicist who specialises in Type Ia supernovae research at Queen's University Belfast. My work focuses on developing and performing Monte Carlo radiative transfer calculations to interpret spectra and light curves of supernovae.

My research contributes to our understanding of the progenitor systems of explosive transients. I'm particularly interested in double-degenerate double-detonation scenarios and how different configurations of helium shells and white dwarf masses change observable characteristics.

Nebular Phase Radiative Transfer Calculations

Investigating the nebular phase of explosion models can be used to understand the progenitors of Type Ia supernovae. This has made been a core aspect of my research to date and has required changes to the detailed microphysics of the radiative transfer code I use.

Investigation of Dynamical Merger Models

Building on findings from my first publication it is clear that Type Ia supernovae models must explore how different helium shell and mass configurations impact the observable characteristics of the new merger scenarios.

Polarisation Signals Of Dynamical Mergers

Investigating the polarisation signals of dynamical merger models can be used to determine how asymmetries deep within explosion models compare with that of observational constraints. This project aims to support or rule out potential progenitor pathways.

Si II velocities

I am particularly interested in the Si II velocities produced by different explosion models and how these can be compared to observational constraints. This allows us to determine how the ejecta in explosion models may be structured.

My work on Type Ia supernovae contributes to our understanding of stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis, with implications for galaxy evolution and cosmology. Key accomplishments include:

  • Developed the capability to conduct the first fully 3D NLTE Nebular simulations for Type Ia supernovae.
  • Published the first 3D Photospheric simulations of dynamical mergers.
  • Conducted the first 3D Polarisation calculations, providing new insights for dynamical mergers.
